NETWORK COMMUNICATION LAB

Cyber-physical and Cybersecurity

  • Farah Kandah, PhD

    I am an IEEE Senior Member and a UC Foundation Tenured Associate Professor in Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) at the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga (UTC) . I am the recipient of the Outstanding Tenured/Tenure-Track Faculty Teaching Computer Science and Engineering Award (2017), the Outstanding Researcher, Computer Science and Engineering award (2016), and the UC Foundation Professorship (2014).

    My research work strongly relies on providing well-designed schemes, techniques, algorithms, and solutions to solve problems and improve the performance of computer networks as well as providing secure transactions and communication among network's users by eliminating and mitigating malicious attacks. My research interests and experience span a wide range of topics in wireless ad-hoc networks from stationary to mobile ad-hoc networks.

  • Farah Kandah, PhD

    I founded the Network communication Laboratory (NCL) at UTC in 2012. The lab leverage expertise on smart communications to support real-time communications in wired and wireless networks. The lab focuses on research in urban science, public safety, smart communications, distributed systems, cybersecurity and Software-defined networks.

  • Farah Kandah, PhD

    Before joining UTC, I worked in research and teaching as I was granted a research and teaching assistantship from the Department of Computer Science at North Dakota State University. Through my PhD, I conducted research with Dr. Kendall Nygard and Dr. Weiyi Zhang in wireless networks, Wireless Mesh Networks, Interference awareness, networks survivability, and security and privacy in wireless networks. In 2005, I received my Master's degree in Computer Science from the University of Jordan. I received my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from the Hashemite University (Jordan) in 2012.
